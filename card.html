Sea Otter

The sea otter is a small marine mammal, native to the coasts of the northern and eastern North Pacific Ocean.
Scientific Name: Enhydra lutris
Predator: Great White Shark
Prey: Sea urchins, Crabs, Clams, Mussels, Octopus, fish, etc.
Life Span: 15 - 20 years
Habitat: Aquatic
Conservation Status: Endangered

Sea otters inhabit coastal waters, usually less than half a mile from shore. Their typical haunts include precipitous rocky shores, barrier reefs, tidewater stones, and dense kelp forests. Sea otters can be found in California, Washington, Canada, Alaska, Russia, and Japan.
